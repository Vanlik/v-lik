This section presents all books/scientific papers/articles that I have read and liked. Please let me know if you have any suggestions on books that I should read next via my email (vanlik.tan[at]gmail.com).



Economics

  • Jean Tirole (2017), Economics for The Common Good
  • Joseph E. Stiglitz and Bruce C. Greenwald, Creating a Learning Society

Biographies

  • Walter Issacson (2004), Benjamin Franklin: An American Life
    • - One of the great book I have ever read. It's a beautifully written essay illustrating the life of Ben Franklin, an enterpreneur, a scientist, an inventor, and the founder of America.
  • T.R. Reid (2007), The Chip: How Two Americans Invented the Microchip and Launched a Revolution
    • - This 2 gentlemen, Robert Noyce of Intel and Jack Kilby of Texas Instruments (TI), invented a unique way to integrate transistors into a tiny little silicon chip that eventually revolutionized the whole electronic and tech industry. [Review]

Natural Science & Technology

  • Rob DeSalle & Susan L. Perkins (2015), Welcome to the Microbiome: Getting to Know the Trillions of Bacteria and Other Microbes In, On, and Around You
    • - A gentle introduction to the world of invisible microbiome and their life. I'm surprised to know that some groups of microbiome has been living with us and had a great impact on human life.
  • James Gleick (2011), The Information: A History, A Theory, A Flood
  • George Gilder (1989), Microcosm: The Quantum Revolution In Economics And Technology

Behavior Science & Psychology

  • Jonah Berger (2020), The Catalyst: How to Change Anyone's Mind
    • - Brilliant piece of strategies that have been used by FBI hostage negotiators to change extremist's mind and get the victim free as well as business practitioners
